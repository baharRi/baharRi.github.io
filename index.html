About me

I believe that well-made design can contribute to create better experiences for anyone. I am firm in my belief that design is crucial for shaping the world into a better place that is more accessible, equitable, and sustainable for every person.

I am eager to collaborate with teams of fellow designers as well as other disciplines in order to craft technology that serves as a tool for empowerment and helpfulness, rather than contributing to exclusion. My areas of interest are interaction design & digitalization. I see value in small details, and I am highly driven to reach my own expectation of perfection with every project I come across.

My Projects

  • AtomIQ interaction

    Interaction layer for crypto investment application

    In the class of IN3010: Transformative Design (Spring 2023), with a focus on social sustainability, my design team partnered with an established team of developers to create a wireframe of the interaction design layer of their upcoming investment app AtomIQ.

  • Svoosh

    Interaction design for android application

    In the class of IN2000: Software Engineering (Spring 2022), I worked in an interdisciplinary team with other informatics students to create an application made to guide the people of Oslo on their bicycling journeys. The app uses APIs from the Norwegian Meteorological Institute and was created through agile software development.

    Within the team I was given the role as head designer, and my responsibilities were to plan interactions with users as well as creating design mockups and implementing the design into the app using the markup language XML. The rest of the application was created using Kotlin, which I also contributed to occasionally.

  • P4L

    Designing, building, & Arduino-programming of P4L the robot

    In the class of IN1060: Use Oriented Design (Spring 2021), in a group with fellow design students, we created P4L: a friendly robot designed to encourage socializing and a sense of responsibility as well as fighting loneliness in a time of crisis and quarantine.

    P4L was created using Arduino in order to implement functionality such as the ability to feed and pet the robot, as well as having it communicate through sounds and movements.
