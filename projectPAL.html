Project description

In the class of IN1060: Use Oriented Design, in a group with fellow design students, we developed P4L: a friendly robot designed to encourage socializing and a sense of responsibility as well as fighting loneliness in a time of crisis and quarantine.

P4L was created using Arduino in order to implement functionality such as the ability to feed and pet the robot, as well as having it communicate through sounds and movements.

Project case:

"Create a pet-figure for the user; something to interact with and care for, in order to fight loneliness during quarantine."

"Boost a feeling of responsibility; give the user the opportunity to feel important in taking care of another being."


Problems

  • The Covid-19 pandemic has changed the social lives of many people, and the population is increasingly at high risk of developing loneliness, depression, and feelings of isolation.
  • Students and otherwise young adults are at higher risk as they often live alone and in non-pet friendly accomodations.

Project objectives

  • Create a series of low-fidelity prototypes as well as a high-fidelity touch-and-feel prototype of the robot concept.
  • Make the robot interactable in a way that brings meaning to the relationship between P4L and the user.
  • Find ways to make the robot appealing for humans.

These objectives are only within the scope of the project timeline.
The goals in the long-run are helping fight loneliness and isolation in society.
Owning a pet can help provide a sense of security, stability and reduce the feeling of loneliness in everyday life. In addition, it is important to keep a sense of meaning and responsibility to one's life, especially in such trying times; something a pet-figure will contribute to.
